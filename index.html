Internal Hackathon

Hackathon & Ideathon Instructions

General Information

  • Teams have to start at as soon as their problem statements have been finalised by the organising team. The Finalisation will be conveyed in the whatsapp group.
  • Date: 15th of October (Showcase & PPT Presentation)
  • Results: 16th of October at Parikalpana 2k25 Event
  • Team size: Minimum 3 and Maximum 5 members
  • Categories: 1. Software statements 2. Hardware statements 3. Ideathon statements
  • Choose only 1 problem statement to solve

Eligibility and Requirements

  • Every registered student of The Neotia University from 1st year to 4th year are eligible to participate in this hackathon and Ideathon.
  • Team formation rules remain as announced by the organizing committee.

Instructions for Hackathon

    Software Problem Statements

    • Provide a proper GUI along with a detailed explanation of the solution.
    • Write complete source code with clear, descriptive comments for all major functions.
    • You are free to use any programming languages, frameworks, or libraries suitable for your solution.

    Hardware Problem Statements

    • Teams may build prototypes using any microcontrollers, sensors, or hardware platforms of their choice (e.g., Arduino, Raspberry Pi, ESP32).
    • Provide a circuit diagram and system flow diagram explaining the hardware setup.
    • Submit a short 1-2 page technical document detailing:
      • Components used and their purpose
      • Design approach and working principle
      • Experimental results or testing methodology
    • Off-the-shelf modules and open-source hardware designs can be used, but original integration and innovation are expected.

    Hackathon PPT Presentation

    • Start with a Title Slide and clearly define the Problem Statement and Proposed Solution.
    • Key Features, Innovation, and the Technology Stack used.
    • Show the Workflow / Architecture and include at least some working demo or prototype .
    • Explain the Impact, Use Cases, Challenges, and Future Scope of your project.
    • Keep the PPT short, visually clear, 8-10 slides max, and limit presentation time to 5-7 minutes.

    Instructions for Ideathon

    Presentation

    • Every team must prepare a powerpoint presentation along with system flow chart or a Block diagram which is to be presented in physical mode in front of the table

    Additional Information

    • For queries or guidance, join the WhatsApp group, link will be provided in the google form. (NOTE: IT'S A MUST)
    • Problem statements are provided separately (click on the name of a problem statement for instructions)
    • The topic descriptions are meant to guide and inspire you, but you are not required to follow them strictly. You can expand, modify, or combine ideas to create more innovative solutions. Your submission must address a real-life problem, be credible. Focus on creativity, practicality, and impact.

    Evaluation Criteria

    For Software Projects

    • Implementation Novelty: Originality and creativity of the software solution.
    • Solution Completeness: How well the application meets the stated problem and functional requirements.
    • Idea Uniqueness: Innovation and distinctiveness of the concept.
    • Code Quality: Readability, maintainability, and documentation of the source code.
    • User Experience: Quality of the GUI, ease of navigation, and overall usability.
    • Presentation: Quality of the PowerPoint (PPT) and clarity of the final project demonstration.

    For Hardware Projects

    • Implementation Novelty: Originality and creativity of the hardware design and integration.
    • Solution Completeness: How effectively the hardware prototype fulfills the problem statement.
    • Idea Uniqueness: Innovation and distinctiveness of the hardware approach.
    • Design & Build Quality: Robustness of the circuit, component selection, and reliability of the prototype.
    • Documentation & Presentation: Clarity of circuit diagrams, system flow, and technical explanations.
    • Presentation: Quality of the PowerPoint (PPT) and clarity of the final project demonstration.
