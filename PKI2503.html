Domain:- Agriculture, Environmental Science, Sustainable Technology

Title:- Farm Waste Upcycling

Background

Agriculture produces massive amounts of lignocellulosic waste (husk, straw, bagasse, peels) which is often burnt (causing pollution) or sent to landfills. This waste is a valuable, renewable resource that can be transformed into higher-value products like biofuel, compost, or eco-friendly materials.

Importance

The project addresses environmental pollution by stopping open burning, creates new revenue streams for farmers and rural economies, and supports a circular bio-economy by replacing chemical inputs or virgin materials with sustainable alternatives.

Challenges

Teams must devise solutions that are low-cost and easy to adopt by local communities, find efficient ways to collect, pre-process, and standardize diverse waste streams (rice husk vs. fruit peel), and ensure the quality and safety of the final upcycled product (e.g., animal feed, fertilizer).
